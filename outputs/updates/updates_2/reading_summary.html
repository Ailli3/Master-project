A Criteria for a experient

For a linear model, the variance of the estimated parameters is related to the experimental design. And a ideal design would provide small variance of both estimated parameters and predicted response.(Atkinson, Donev, and Tobias 2007). A experiment result provided by Atkinson shows that a design is good for one property may do worse in another. Therefore, a design is optimal for some criteria may not be optimal under others. So a optimal design is decided by a chosen criteria.

Alphabet Optimality

A Brief Introduction of Alphabet Optimality

Most criteria come under the heading of “alphabet optimality” because the criterion’s name is letter form the alphabet.(Russell 2018) Here is some commonly use introduced by Russell:

  • \(A\)- minimises the average of variance of the parameter estimates,
  • \(D\)- minimises volume of a confidence hyperellipsoid for the parameters,(?)
  • \(D_s\) minimises volume of a confidence hyperellipsoid for a subset of the parameters taking into account the presence of the remaining parameters.(?)

\(A\)-optimality

A design denoted as \(d\) is \(A\)-optimal among all possible designs if it has the minimum value of the average of the variances of the parameter estimates.

It has little meaning when we consider a linear model, and so it is unlikely that one would wish to choose a design that minimises this average.(Russell 2018)

But if we are dealing with indicator variables indicating different level of treatment factor. Under the circumstance we want to compare the effect of several different treatments, \(A\)-optimality helps to minimise the average of the variance of the effect of the pairwise treatment differences.

Now consider a randomized complete block design. The statistical model for the response variable \(Y_ij\), from the plot in block \(j\) that effected by treatment \(i\) is \[ Y_{ij}= \mu+ \tau_i +\rho_j+E_{ij} \quad i=1,\dots,t;j=1,\dots,b \] where \(\mu\) is the overall mean, \(\tau_i\) is the effect of the \(i\)th treatment, \(\rho_j\) is the effect of the \(j\) block and \(E_{ij}\) is random error.

There are \(t+b+1\) parameter for this model. So we may use indicator variables to rewrite it as a general linear model.

\[ Y_{ij}= \mu + \sum_{i=1}^{t}I_i^{t}\tau_i + \sum_{j=1}^{b}I_j^b\rho_j+E_{ij},\quad i=1,\dots,t;j=1,\dots,b \]

Here \(\mu\), \(\tau_i\), \(\rho_j\) and \(E_{ij}\) are the same above. and for \(I_i^{t}\) and \(I_j^b\) we have

$$ I_i^{t}={

\[\begin{array}{rcl} 1 & given & ith & treatment\\ 0 & otherwise \end{array}\]

.

I_j^{b}={

\[\begin{array}{rcl} 1 & in & jth & block\\ 0 & otherwise \end{array}\]

. $$

and the A-optimality criterion is

\[ \frac{1}{t(t-1)}{\sum_{i=1}^{t}\sum_{j=1}^{t}}_{i\neq j}var(\hat{\tau_i}-\hat{\tau_j})^2 \]

(Russell 2018)

That is, The A-optimal design minimizes the average of the variance of the pair wise treatment differences.

For \(var(\hat{\tau_i}-\hat{\tau_j})^2\) we may use variance-covariance matrix of linear regression model. \[ Var(\hat{\beta})=\sigma^2(X^TX)^{-1} \] We are using notations from linear regression model. Here \(\hat{\beta}=(\mu,\tau_1,\tau_2,\dots,\tau_t,\rho_1,\rho_2,\dots,\rho_b)\), and \(X=(I_1^{t},I_2^{t},\dots,I_t^{t},I_1^{b},I_2^{b},\dots,I_b^{b})\)

\(Var(\hat{\beta})\) is a variance-covariance in which the diagonal elements provide the variance of each coefficient, while the off-diagonal elements provide the covariance between different coefficients. so for calculating \(var(\hat{\tau_i}-\hat{\tau_j})^2\) we have \[ var(\hat{\tau_i}-\hat{\tau_j})=var(\hat{\tau_i})+var(\hat{\tau_j})-2cov(\hat{\tau_i},\tau_j) \]
